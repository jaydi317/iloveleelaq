THE 1890s
🚂

The first movie audiences
ran from the screen.

In 1896, the Lumière brothers screened a film of a train arriving at a station. The audience — grown adults — leapt from their chairs and fled the theater because they thought a real train was coming through the wall.

THEIR REACTION
"It's going to kill us!"

We laugh at that now. But they weren't stupid — they had never seen anything like it. Their brains had no reference point for images moving on a wall.
